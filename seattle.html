The City of Olympia is the capitol of the State of Washington.

Until the city of Olympia was colonized by Europeans, it was inhabited by the Squaxin Island Tribe ("People of the Water") and the Coast Salish People. The city was incorporated as a town on January 28, 1859.

The city of Olympia is ranked only 25th largest city of the State of Washington with a population of 55,774. Source

The median income of the city of Olympia was $73,816 in 2022 compared to a state average of $91,306.

Olympia is an urban city with the following geographic makeup:

  • City - 20.09 sq mi (52.02 km2)
  • Land - 18.23 sq mi (47.20 km2)
  • Water - 1.87 sq mi (4.82 km2)
Source

Olympia is located at the southern end of the Puget Sound in Thurston County.
